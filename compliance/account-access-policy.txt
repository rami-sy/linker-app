Account Access Maintenance Policy — Linker App (linker.land)
Date: May 25, 2026

OVERVIEW:
This policy defines how the Linker App manages, reviews, and revokes
administrative access to systems where Meta Platform Data is stored.

ACCOUNT MAINTENANCE REQUIREMENTS:

[1] Revoke access that is no longer required
    When an access grant becomes unnecessary (e.g., a task is completed,
    a role changes, or a service is decommissioned), access is revoked
    immediately by removing the corresponding SSH public key from:
    /root/.ssh/authorized_keys
    Command used: sed -i '/key-identifier/d' /root/.ssh/authorized_keys

[2] Revoke access that is no longer being used
    All SSH keys are reviewed at least once every 12 months.
    Keys that have not been used within the past 12 months are removed.
    Last login time is checked via: last -a | head -20
    Unused keys are identified and removed from authorized_keys.

[3] Revoke access promptly when a person leaves the organization
    If a developer, contractor, or collaborator departs, their SSH key
    is removed from /root/.ssh/authorized_keys immediately upon departure.
    This is a manual process triggered by the departure event.
    Target revocation time: within 24 hours of departure.

CURRENT ACCESS INVENTORY:
- 1 active SSH key (sole developer: Rami Sweyri)
- No shared accounts or service accounts with Platform Data access
- Access is reviewed annually (next review: May 2027)

HOW REVOCATION IS INITIATED:
Access is revoked by editing /root/.ssh/authorized_keys and removing
the specific key entry. This immediately prevents future SSH logins
using that key without requiring a service restart.
